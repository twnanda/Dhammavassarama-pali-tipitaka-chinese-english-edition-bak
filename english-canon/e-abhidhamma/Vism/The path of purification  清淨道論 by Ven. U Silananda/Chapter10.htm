†† A person who wants to attain these immaterial states or brahm» vih»ras must have already attained the four or five form jh»nas or material jh»nas. Without getting those jh»nas one will not be able to attain the immaterial states.

†† ìOne who wants firstly to develop the base consisting of boundless space sees in gross physical matter danger through the wielding of sticks, etc., because of the words.î In order to go to the immaterial states or in order to attain the formless jh»nas one needs to find fault with matter. †† Then he sees danger in it through the thousand afflictions beginning with eye disease and so on. When we have this physical body we may quarrel with some other person. †† In order to surmount that he enters upon the fourth form (r|p»vacara) jh»na with any of the nine kasi”as beginning with the earth kasi”a and omitting the limited space kasi”a. †† ìNow although he has already surmounted gross physical matter by means of the fourth jh»na of the fine material sphere, nevertheless he still wants also to surmount the kasi”a materiality since it is the counterpart of the former.î When he is in the jh»nas, he is said to be out of physical matters, but still he wants to surmount the kasi”a materiality.

†† Let us take the fourth jh»na. If he practices with earth kasi”a meditation, then his fourth jh»na takes earth kasi”a as object. †† It is like a person who sees a snake or who is pursued by a snake and he is afraid.† Even after that when he sees a rope or a crack in the earth, he is afraid of that. In the same way since this meditator hates matter, he hates the kasi”a materiality also. So he wants to surmount the kasi”a matter too. The simile given is that it is like the man pursued by the snake.

†† The other similes are the dog attacked by a boar and the pis»ca goblin and the timid man. Actually it means a man afraid of pis»cas. †† Even those who have never seen a ghost in their life are afraid of ghosts because they have heard from other people about ghosts. A person who is afraid of ghosts may be afraid of a tree stump or even his own shadow.
